# Deposit Dispute Playbook

Use this reference when the user is mainly asking about 押金不退, 房东扣押金, 退租押金, or disputed move-out deductions.

## Minimum Facts

- City and district.
- Lease start/end dates and actual move-out date.
- Monthly rent and deposit amount.
- Deposit return clause and deduction clause.
- Landlord or agency deduction reason.
- Whether the user has an itemized deduction list.
- Move-in photos, move-out photos, inspection records, repair chats, payment proof.

## First-Pass Assessment

Label the case as:

- `tenant_favorable`: clear payment proof, move-out condition evidence, landlord has vague or unsupported deductions.
- `mixed_risk`: some evidence on both sides, unclear inspection record, reasonable minor deductions possible.
- `landlord_favorable`: clear tenant-caused damage, signed settlement, or missing tenant evidence.
- `evidence_insufficient`: no contract/payment proof/photos or deduction facts are unclear.

## Evidence Priority

1. Signed lease and deposit clause.
2. Deposit transfer or receipt.
3. Move-in and move-out photos/videos.
4. Chat records where landlord admits condition, repairs, deposit, or deduction reason.
5. Itemized deduction quote, repair invoice, cleaning bill, or inspection record.
6. Witnesses, property management records, or third-party repair assessment.

## Practical Escalation

Start with a written demand letter that sets a specific amount and deadline. If no response, prepare a complaint package for 12315, local housing authority, community mediation, arbitration if the contract requires it, or court filing for small claims where appropriate.
